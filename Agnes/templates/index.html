Our Solution

Our water quality prediction system involves a hybrid model integrating Support Vector Machines (SVM), Random Forest networks. This ensemble approach leverages the strengths of each model to enhance prediction accuracy and robustness. Historical water quality data is preprocessed and fed into the hybrid model, which independently processes the data to generate predictions based on its learning patterns and characteristics. The results are then aggregated to produce a final prediction or estimation of water quality parameters. By combining multiple machine learning techniques, the solution aims to provide reliable insights into water quality, facilitating informed decision-making in water resource management and environmental conservation efforts.

IoT Hardware

The IoT hardware utilized in our system comprises standalone pH and turbidity sensors designed for water quality monitoring applications. These sensors provide local readings of pH levels and turbidity in water bodies. Users manually input these readings into the system for further processing and analysis. While the sensors lack built-in connectivity for automatic data transmission, they offer portability and ease of use, making them suitable for field-based monitoring and sampling activities. By integrating these sensors into our system, we enable users to collect accurate water quality data and contribute to informed decision-making in water resource management efforts.
